Luxor Library


Opening

The library opened on 19/5/2007.

Location

The Library and Cultural Heritage Center is located at the northern entrance to Luxor City on Karnak Temple Street. It is located in the most prestigious location in the city, about 200 meters away from the banks of the Nile. The area behind the library was developed by implementing the first phase of Rams Road.

Space

The total area of ​​the library is 2339 square meters.

Library components

  1. Cultural Heritage Hall: It is a panorama showroom of the cultural heritage and archaeological monuments of Egypt to Luxor before and after the development and it contains 218 seats and 9 data show devices.

  2. Knights of the Sky: Knights of the Sky: It is a hall that highlights the great scientific progress of Arab inventions that preceded the world, especially in the field of monitoring and navigation.

  3. Stereoscopic display: Stereoscopic display: It consists of three rooms dedicated to seeing the shows in three dimensions of the live and stereo presentation of the cultural heritage using special glasses that in turn embody the display for the viewer as if it were on the ground.

  4. Eternal Egypt: Eternal Egypt: It is a corner equipped with a set of computers that allows viewing the Eternal Egypt website and provides the visitor with all the information related to the various ancient Egyptian civilizations and helps him to provide his historical information. There is also a cafeteria in the basement to serve visitors.

It includes a foyer, entrance hall, information and security, a corner for ongoing briefing, which is equipped with a set of computers, the administration office, and a number of halls:

  1. Egyptology Hall: It is the first hall in Egypt for Egyptology to serve Egyptian and foreign scholars and researchers in the history and civilization of ancient Egypt in various languages. It also contains a set of pictures of Luxor, which dates back more than a hundred years. It contains about 10,000 information containers specialized in the history of Egypt through the ages, especially with regard to the Pharaonic era.

  2. Map Hall: It includes a set of paper and digital maps of Luxor and its archaeological sites, as well as maps of all parts of Egypt.

  3. Senior Information Hall (1): The hall accommodates 35 people.

First Floor:

It consists of an adult information room (2), a children's information room, a green corner, a children's activities hall, a computer education room, a language teaching room, and an Internet corner for adults and children.

Second Floor:

It includes lecture halls and a training center.

Working Hours

The library operates from 10-9 pm and the weekly rest is on Tuesdays.
